Arabic Chicken & Potatoes

Exotic spices lift chicken and potatoes in this complete Middle Eastern meal-in-a-tray!

Ingredients

  • 5 x 250 g bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • Extra-virgin olive oil spray, or 2 tsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 kg potatoes, scrubbed clean, cut into 7 mm thick slices
  • 1 bunch Dutch carrots, scrubbed clean (10–12 carrots)
  • 2 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 tsp sumac powder
  • 1 tsp cooking salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 large garlic clove, crushed
  • ½ tsp dried thyme
  • ½ tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp sumac powder
  • ½ tsp white sesame seeds
  • 1 tsp cooking salt
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • 3 tbsp plain yoghurt (or sour cream)
  • Handful of coriander or parsley (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C (180°C fan-forced). Line a large baking tray with foil and baking paper.
  2. Place the potatoes and carrots in a large bowl. Drizzle with olive oil, sumac, salt, and pepper. Toss well to coat, then spread onto the tray, leaving space for the chicken.
  3. Mix all the chicken spice paste ingredients. Add chicken and rub the mixture thoroughly into each piece. Place chicken on the tray with the vegetables.
  4. Bake for 45–50 minutes, rotating the tray halfway, until chicken is golden and cooked through.
  5. Rest for 5 minutes before serving. Garnish with coriander or parsley, and use tray juices as sauce.
